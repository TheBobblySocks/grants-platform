# Ending Homelessness in Communities Fund — Prospectus

> Source: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/ending-homelessness-in-communities-fund-prospectus
> Retrieved: 2026-04-16

---

## Overview

The MHCLG administers a £37 million competitive grant supporting voluntary, community, and faith sector organisations across England. The fund spans three years (2026–2029) to strengthen homelessness prevention and support services.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

**Organisation type:**
- Registered charities, Community Interest Companies, Community Benefit Societies, or Parochial Church Councils
- Operating in England only
- Maximum annual income: £5 million in most recent financial year
- Must have delivered direct services to people experiencing or at risk of rough sleeping for a minimum of 3 years
- Cannot be individuals, sole traders, public bodies, or profit-distributing entities

**Application requirements:**
- Single application per organisation (can participate as partner in others' bids)
- Letter of endorsement from local authority homelessness/rough sleeping lead officer required
- Partnership applications allowed with one lead organisation accountable for funds

---

## Funding Structure

**Available amounts:**
- Revenue: £50,000–£200,000 annually for up to 3 years
- Capital: £50,000–£200,000 (Year 1 or Year 2 only, single year only)
- Both types can be requested simultaneously in one application

**Timeline:**

| Milestone | Date |

## Eligible and Ineligible Uses

**Revenue may fund:**
- Staff salaries and project activities
- Running costs (premises rent, utilities)
- Minor refurbishments and equipment
- Workforce development
- Overhead contributions
- Immigration advice (IAA-regulated)

**Capital may fund:**
- Property acquisition and significant upgrading
- IT systems and fixtures
- Significant transformation works

**Cannot fund:**
- Hotel/B&B accommodation, communal sleeping/night shelter facilities
- Political or exclusively religious activity
- VAT (if recoverable)
- Statutory obligations already provided by local authority
- Lobbying or political campaigning
- Temporary structures
- Costs incurred before funding agreement is signed
- Projects previously funded through the Night Shelter Transformation Fund (capital only)

---

## Assessment Criteria

Applications are scored on a 0–3 scale across seven weighted criteria. Maximum total: 300 points.

**Scoring scale:** 0 = unacceptable, 1 = satisfactory, 2 = good, 3 = excellent

| # | Criterion | Weight | Weighted max |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Skills and experience | 10% | 30 |
| 2 | Proposal Part 1 — challenge identification with local evidence | 10% | 30 |
| 3 | Proposal Part 2 — project alignment to fund objectives | 30% | 90 |
| 4 | Deliverability Part 1 — milestones, planning, governance | 25% | 75 |
| 5 | Deliverability Part 2 — risk identification and mitigation | 5% | 15 |
| 6 | Cost and value — itemised budget, value for money | 10% | 30 |
| 7 | Outcomes and impact — measurable results, system strengthening | 10% | 30 |

**Auto-reject rule:** Any criterion scoring 0 results in rejection regardless of total score.

---

## Three Core Fund Objectives

1. **Community-based support** — expand casework, volunteer coordination, crisis prevention, referral pathways, single-unit accommodation provision
2. **Day services enhancement** — improve physical infrastructure, co-located services, staff development, long-term rough sleeper programmes
3. **Recovery support** — sustain tenancies, provide employment support, mentoring, peer support post-accommodation

---

## Capital Funding — Readiness Requirements

**Year 1 capital applicants must demonstrate:**
- Planning permission secured or clearly scheduled
- Tenure security confirmed
- Professional advice obtained
- Qualified contractors identified
- Project deliverable by 31 March 2027

Exact quotes and planning permissions are not required at application stage, but Year 1 delivery demands higher readiness evidence.

---

## Partnership Applications

- One lead organisation must meet all eligibility requirements
- Partner organisations do not require individual eligibility verification
- A Memorandum of Understanding is required if the application is successful
- One lead application maximum per organisation

---

## Post-Award Obligations

Successful recipients must:
- Submit annual Statement of Grant Usage signed by an auditor
- Comply with Grant Funding Agreement terms
- Participate in monitoring and evaluation activities
- Acknowledge UK Government funding in marketing materials
- Comply with the Subsidy Control Act 2022 and Equality Act 2010
