## Overview

The Pride in Place Programme is a flagship UK government initiative designed to empower local communities to shape their neighbourhoods. It represents "one of the largest investments in deprived neighbourhoods for a generation" with up to £5.8 billion distributed across 284 communities over 10 years.

Administered by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG).

---

## Key Facts

**Funding:**
- Up to £20 million per place over 10 years
- Total investment: up to £5.8 billion
- Un-competed funding — not a bidding/application process

**Eligibility:**
- 284 pre-selected communities across Great Britain
  - 75 Phase 1 places
  - 169 Phase 2 places
  - 40 additional places announced 5 February 2026
- Focused on areas with highest deprivation and weakest social infrastructure

## Three Strategic Objectives

1. **Stronger Communities** — rebuilding relationships and social trust
2. **Thriving Places** — revitalising neighbourhoods and public services
3. **Taking Back Control** — empowering residents with agency over their futures

---

## Programme Structure

### Neighbourhood Boards

Each eligible community is governed by a Neighbourhood Board, which:
- Is led by an independent Chair approved by the local MP and council
- Includes residents, businesses, community leaders, and elected representatives
- Must have more residents than elected officials
- Develops a 10-year **Pride in Place Plan** through community engagement

### Pride in Place Plans

Each board submits a long-term plan covering priorities, interventions, and governance. Boards can invest in interventions from an indicative list provided by MHCLG, or propose alternatives with MHCLG approval.

---

## Key Principles

- **Community-led** — residents at the centre of decision-making
- **Flexible** — boards choose their own interventions within the framework
- **Long-term** — 10-year funding horizon to enable lasting change
- **Supportive** — government provides capacity support, not just money

---

## Transition to Sustainability

By year three, boards must move toward community-led delivery models to ensure impact extends beyond the 10-year funding period. Eligible delivery models include:
- Cooperatives
- Charities
- Community Interest Companies (CICs)
- Established local organisations

---

## Support Provided

- Dedicated Communities Delivery Unit officials assigned to each area
- Network for Neighbourhoods for sharing best practices across places
- Capacity funding for community engagement and organisational development
- Technical assistance on planning, architecture, and place management
